1. Number of Active credit cards. An average of 2-3 active credit cards improves your score. Zero credit cards or many credit cards reduces your score as this does not show a greater credit risk

2. Annual gross salary. The more you earn, the more likely you're able to meet for financial obligations as they fall due. Higher annual income will increase your credit score

3. Payment history for loans and credit cards. This includes the number and severity of late payments and overdue credit accounts. Late payments will have a greater negative effect on your credit score than any other single factor.

4. Credit utilization. The total amount of outstanding debt you have relative to your credit limits is also known as your credit utilization ratio, and it plays a major role in determining your scores. To avoid negative effects on your credit scores, experts recommend keeping outstanding balances at or below 30% of each card's borrowing limit, but the lower your utilization, the better.

5. New credit accounts you've recently opened. Opening several accounts could represent a greater credit risk. Why? Because people tend to do so when they are experiencing cash flow problems or planning to take on lots of new debt. The negative effect of this factor tends to fade within a few months, as long as you keep up with all required payments.

6. Length of credit history. Lenders value borrowers with experience handling debt responsibly. A lengthy record of responsible credit usage (with minimal late or missed payments) tends to elevate scores more than a comparably short history of credit usage.

7. Total limit on credit cards. The higher the credit limit at your bank shows that you have a good credit history and this increases your credit score.

8. bankruptcy. Any record of bankruptcy, lowers your credit score greatly. Bankruptcy should be avoided as financial institutions can nolonger trust you with their finances

About Fin Cred Credit Scores

FinCred® is an app created to helping our users improve their financial wellbeing. The application is used for users to check their credit worthiness. • Users provide their credit history and the information is used to estimate their credit score. • The credit score ranges between 300-850 and graded with highest score attaining an excellent remark and the least scores attaining an adverse remark. The higher the score, the better a borrower looks to potential lenders. • FinCred also provides recommendations to users on financial institutions with affordable interest rates to approach based on the credit score outcome.
